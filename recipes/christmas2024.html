Pot Roast Ingredients:

  • 1.5 kg beef chuck roast (shoulder cut)
  • 1 cup water mixed with beef bouillon
  • 250 ml red wine, or more if you like
  • 1 whole head of garlic
  • 1 or 2 carrorts, roughly chopped
  • Quarter wedge of celery, roughly chopped
  • 1 or 2 large onions, roughly chopped
  • 1 or 2 bay leaves
  • 1 or 2 sprigs of rosemary
  • 1 or 2 sprigs of thyme
  • 1 or 2 sprigs of parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for searing
  • A little apple cider vinegar

Instructions:

  1. Season the chuck roast really well on all sides with salt and pepper.
  2. In a large pan (or like I like to do in the roasting pan) heat some oil, and sear the roast on all sides in high heat and set it aside.
  3. In the same pan, saute the onions, carrots, and celery until they're soft.
  4. Add garlic to the pan for a few seconds. Make sure it doesn't burn.
  5. Put the roast back in the pan, add the bay leaves, rosemary, thyme, and parsley.
  6. Pour the water and beef bouillon mixture and the red wine in the pan.
  7. Cover the pan and let it simmer in the oven at 150°C 3-4 hours, or until the meat is tender.
  8. Remove the meat from the pan and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing.
  9. In a small pan melt some butter and add flour. Stir for a couple minutes together.
  10. Strain the sauce into the small pan with the butter and flour mixture. Incorporate everything well together until the sauce is smooth and velvety.
  11. Season the sauce with salt and pepper.
  12. Serve the meat with the sauce and the sides.
  13. Enjoy!
